== Episode summary (AI-generated)

Milestone & Episode 300 Plans::
* *1,000 Subscribers:* The hosts kick off the show by celebrating passing 1,000 subscribers on YouTube, reflecting on their purely organic growth since starting the channel back in 2012.

* *Live Stream for Episode 300:* With Episode 300 right around the corner, they plan to host a live stream—a format they haven't done in a very long time.
They are looking for audience feedback on whether it should be a casual Q&A session, a VR gameplay stream, or an open forum.

Debriefing the Mel Kirk (Zen Studios) Interview::
* *Reaction and Industry Focus:* They dissect the feedback from their recent 45-minute interview with Mel Kirk from Zen Studios.
They note that other YouTubers and podcasts heavily fixated on a brief 5-minute segment regarding *AtGames*, completely overlooking several hints Mel dropped about future table releases.

* *AtGames Tensions & Body Language:* Chris and Jared highlight Mel's visibly unhappy body language regarding AtGames.

* *The New Underpowered TMNT Cabinet:* They discuss AtGames' new _Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles_ HD+ cabinet.
While it upgrades to the same Rockchip board found in the 4K cabinet and includes a DMD screen, they caution that the hardware remains technically underpowered.
They point out that AtGames' press releases only specify bringing "select" Zen titles, noting that Williams tables are likely too resource-heavy to emulate smoothly on it.

Jared's "Gottlieb Intervention" (New Physical Machine Additions::
* *The New Additions:* Jared explains the episode's title by revealing that he bought two classic physical pinball machines: Gottlieb's *Haunted House* and *Black Hole*.

* *Restoration Strategy:* Unlike his previous waterlogged "container jobs" imported from Africa, these machines are in decent physical shape.
Jared explains that he plans to use Pascal Pi80 replacement boards rather than trying to fix original, finicky electronics.
He also has to custom-build 12 new Wollftech displays over his Christmas break.

* *Haunted House vs. Black Hole:* Chris admits he isn't a fan of _Haunted House_ due to its annoying mini-playfields, scissor flippers, and sound layout.
However, they both marvel at the early 1980s engineering that allowed Gottlieb to run _Haunted House's_ complex 8-flipper, 3-level rule set on a single system board using logic-switching relays.

Technical Cabinet Troubleshooting & Media Mini-Reviews::
* *The Star Wars Cab Project:* Chris shares his frustration trying to update his 2017 physical _Star Wars_ pinball cabinet.
Upon trying to update Steam, he discovered the computer inside runs a 32-bit version of Windows 10, which Steam is dropping support for.
Upgrading to 64-bit is proving tricky due to potential driver incompatibilities with the cabinet's custom force-feedback/solenoid boards.

* *Movie Review - Kill Bill: The Whole Bloody Affair:* Chris wraps up the episode sharing his mild disappointment seeing the combined cut of _Kill Bill_ in theaters.
While the extended 7-minute anime backstory for O-Ren Ishii and the full-color House of Blue Leaves fight were cool, the second half of the film was completely unchanged, lacking the deeper narrative re-editing he had spent years hoping for.
